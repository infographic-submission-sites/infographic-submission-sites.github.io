Infographic Submission Sites: Complete SEO Guide for Better Backlinks and Brand Visibility

Infographics have become one of the most effective content formats for sharing complex information in a simple, engaging way. They combine visuals, data, and storytelling, making content easier to understand and more likely to be shared across websites and social media.

Creating a high-quality infographic is only the first step. To maximize its reach, marketers distribute it through infographic submission sites, where businesses, bloggers, and publishers discover visual content worth sharing. This strategy not only increases exposure but can also generate valuable backlinks, referral traffic, and brand recognition.

When combined with other off-page SEO strategies like guest posting sites, infographic promotion creates a stronger and more diverse backlink profile. Instead of relying on a single link-building method, businesses can attract links naturally from multiple sources while expanding their online presence.

This guide explains how infographic submission works, why it matters for SEO, and how to create an effective promotion strategy that delivers long-term results.

Why Infographics Matter for SEO

Search engines reward content that provides value to users. Infographics help achieve this because they present information in a visually appealing and easy-to-digest format.

Unlike lengthy text articles, visual content captures attention within seconds. Readers often spend more time engaging with attractive graphics, which improves user experience and increases sharing opportunities.

Benefits of Using Infographics

Businesses invest in infographic marketing because it offers several advantages:

  • Simplifies complex topics
  • Improves user engagement
  • Increases social media shares
  • Generates high-quality backlinks
  • Strengthens brand authority
  • Encourages publishers to reference your content
  • Supports content marketing campaigns
  • Improves website visibility

According to multiple content marketing studies, visual content receives significantly higher engagement than text-only content, making infographics a valuable asset for digital marketers.

What Are Infographic Submission Sites?

Infographic submission sites are online platforms where individuals and businesses can upload, publish, and share infographic content with a broader audience.

These websites organize graphics into categories such as:

  • Digital Marketing
  • Technology
  • Health
  • Finance
  • Education
  • Business
  • Lifestyle
  • Travel

How Do They Work?

The process is relatively simple.

  1. Design an informative infographic.
  2. Publish it on your website.
  3. Upload it to submission platforms.
  4. Add a short description.
  5. Include your website URL as the original source.
  6. Share it through social media and email outreach.
  7. Monitor backlinks and referral traffic.

Over time, bloggers and publishers may embed your infographic in their own articles while crediting your website.

This creates additional backlinks without requiring manual outreach for every link.

Characteristics of a High-Quality Infographic

Not every infographic earns backlinks. The most successful ones focus on solving a real problem while presenting accurate information in an engaging format.

Include Original Data

Unique information attracts significantly more attention than recycled content.

Examples include:

  • Survey results
  • Industry research
  • Customer insights
  • Market trends
  • Case study findings

Original data makes your infographic more trustworthy and increases citation opportunities.

Keep the Design Clean

Good design improves readability.

Follow these principles:

  • Use consistent colors.
  • Maintain adequate spacing.
  • Limit font styles.
  • Use readable typography.
  • Highlight important numbers.
  • Avoid clutter.

Remember that simplicity usually outperforms overly decorative designs.

Tell a Story

Rather than listing random facts, structure your infographic logically.

  • Problem
  • Statistics
  • Analysis
  • Solution
  • Key Takeaways

Storytelling keeps readers engaged from beginning to end.

Make It Mobile-Friendly

More users now browse content on smartphones than desktops.

Design your infographic with:

  • Readable font sizes
  • Vertical layouts
  • Optimized image dimensions
  • Compressed file sizes

Mobile-friendly graphics encourage longer engagement and easier sharing.

How to Create an Effective Infographic Promotion Strategy

Creating a visually appealing infographic is only half the job. The real results come from promoting it strategically. Businesses that consistently earn backlinks and referral traffic don't rely on a single submission—they combine multiple promotion methods to maximize visibility.

Step 1: Start with Keyword Research

Before designing your infographic, identify a topic your audience is actively searching for.

Look for keywords that:

  • Have informational search intent
  • Solve a specific problem
  • Include current statistics or trends
  • Can be explained visually

The more targeted your topic, the more useful your infographic becomes.

Step 2: Publish the Infographic on Your Website First

Always publish the original version on your own website before distributing it elsewhere.

Your page should include:

  • A compelling title
  • A detailed introduction
  • Supporting content explaining the infographic
  • Optimized images
  • Internal links
  • Social sharing buttons

This helps search engines identify your website as the original source.

Step 3: Write a Unique Description

  • Summarize the infographic.
  • Explain who it helps.
  • Mention the key takeaway.
  • Include relevant keywords naturally.
  • Add a clear source link.

Unique descriptions improve credibility and reduce duplicate content issues.

Step 4: Submit to Quality Directories

Rather than submitting everywhere, prioritize websites that are:

  • Relevant to your niche
  • Frequently updated
  • Trusted by search engines
  • Used by real audiences

A smaller number of quality submissions often delivers better SEO results than hundreds of low-quality listings.

Step 5: Promote Across Multiple Channels

Don't stop after submitting your infographic.

  • LinkedIn posts
  • Pinterest boards
  • Facebook groups
  • X (Twitter)
  • Email newsletters
  • Industry forums
  • Community websites

Each channel exposes your content to a different audience and increases the chances of earning backlinks.

An infographic should educate first and promote second. Too many logos, advertisements, or sales messages reduce credibility and discourage sharing.

Ignoring Mobile Users

  • Mobile devices
  • Tablets
  • Desktop screens

Using Outdated Statistics

Review your infographic regularly and update:

  • Statistics
  • Charts
  • Trends
  • Sources
  • References

Poor Visual Hierarchy

  • The topic

Forgetting SEO Optimization

  • Descriptive image file names
  • Image alt text
  • Structured headings

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What are infographic submission sites?

They are online platforms where businesses and individuals publish infographics to increase visibility, referral traffic, and backlink opportunities.

Are infographic submissions good for SEO?

Yes. High-quality infographic submissions can improve brand awareness, attract natural backlinks, and drive referral traffic when used correctly.

Do infographic submission sites provide backlinks?

Many platforms allow source URLs. Some links are nofollow, while others may lead to valuable editorial backlinks when publishers reuse your infographic.

How do I create an infographic that attracts backlinks?

  • Solve a real problem.
  • Use original research.
  • Create a clean design.
  • Make it mobile-friendly.
  • Tell a compelling story.

How often should I submit infographics?

Quality matters more than quantity. Publishing one well-researched infographic every month or quarter is generally effective.

Can beginners use infographic submission?

Absolutely. Bloggers, freelancers, startups, and small businesses can all use infographic marketing to grow their online visibility.

Final Thoughts
